The Last Halloween

Project Overview

The Last Halloween is a game that me and a team of other students developed as a part of the MSU Game Design and development minor. I was the project manager for my team, but filled in programming in some areas

  • Programs Used
  • -Unity
  • -C#
  • -Hack n' Plan
  • -Gitlab Version control
  • -Microsoft Powerpoint

Team Experience

This game was not my first experience working in a team, however it has been the longest I've worked on a team project. Not only that it was my first experience being a project manager. As a project manager I was in charge of organizing sprints, delegating tasks, and ensuring that the team was on track to complete the game within the two month time limit. This experience taught me a lot about leadership and communication within a team setting. I also learned a great deal about scope and time management, as well as the agile development process

Swiss Army Knife

This project taught me the value of being a versatile team memner, especially when in a producer role. Due to the small nature of our team and the limited time we had to complete the project, I had to fill in wherever I was needed. This included programming, designing, and testing when our leads were focusing on more important features. Namely I created the mini-map and summary page UI while our programmer and designers were pre-occupied

Where We Succeeded

Our team was able to develop a functional game within the time constraints, effectively collaborate using version control, and implement some highly technical features such as our fully procedurally generated levels.

Where I Can Improve

We had to cut a lot of features, and ran out of scope a lot quicker than expected. In future projects I'll focus on being more reasonable when planning, and managing scope more effectively.
